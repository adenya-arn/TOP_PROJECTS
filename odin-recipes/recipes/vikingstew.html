Viking Stew

Recipe Description


Credit: Allrecipes/ John Mitzewich

This Viking stew, made with meat, berries, mushrooms, plenty of fresh herbs, and root vegetables typically available to the Vikings, is a seemingly primitive dish with a remarkably sophisticated flavor.
It works with any meat, so if you cannot hunt for wild boar, do not be dismayedâ€”you can still make it!

Recipe Info

  • Prep Time: 30 min
  • Cook Time: 2hrs
  • Total Time: 2hrs 30min
  • Servings: 6

Ingredients

  1. 4 1/2 pounds boneless pork shoulder roast, cut i
    2. 2- to 3-inch chunks
  2. 3 teaspoons kosher salt, or to taste
  3. 1/4 cup honey
  4. 1 cup fresh blackberries, or other fresh berries
  5. 2 tablespoons lingonberry jam
  6. 6 cups cold water
  7. 3 cloves garlic, peeled
  8. 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  9. 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  10. 1 yellow onion, cut into large dice
  11. 2 leeks, washed thoroughly, cut into 1-inch pieces
  12. 10 large brown mushrooms, halved
  13. 1 small green cabbage, cut into 1-inch pieces
  14. 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  15. 2 fennel bulbs, halved and cut into 1/4-inch strips
  16. 1/4 cup torn cilantro leaves
  17. 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  18. 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves

Directions

Step 1

Add pork to a large soup pot or Dutch oven.
Add salt, honey, berries, lingonberry jam, and cold water, and turn the heat to high.

Step 2

While stew is coming to a boil, add garlic, dry thyme, and ground coriander.
When mixture comes to a rolling boil, reduce heat to low, and simmer gently for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 3

Stir in onion, leeks, and mushrooms, and simmer until meat is almost, but not quite fork tender, about 45 minutes more.

Step 4

Stir in cabbage, carrots, and fennel; continue to simmer until vegetables and meat are very tender, about 30 minutes.

Step 5

Season to taste with salt.
Serve with fresh herbs scattered over the top.
